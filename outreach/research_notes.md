# Outreach Research Notes — HUD Affordable Housing Compliance Tracker

## Target Categories

### Potential Clients
**Yardi Systems** — Dominates affordable housing property management software (RENTCafe Affordable). Their clients are the exact management companies we profile. REAC cross-link data would enhance their compliance monitoring products. Tony Pizur leads data products.

**National Housing Compliance (NHC)** — Specializes in LIHTC compliance monitoring for state Housing Finance Agencies. David Brumberg (CEO) would understand the value of linking LIHTC allocations to inspection outcomes.

**Novogradac & Company** — The industry's leading LIHTC accounting/consulting firm. They already publish LIHTC databases and reports. Cross-linked REAC data would be a natural extension of their offerings.

**Affordable Housing Investors (AHI)** — LIHTC syndicator that needs property performance data for portfolio monitoring. Management company score profiles directly inform their investment decisions.

### Research Collaborators
**Ingrid Gould Ellen (NYU Furman Center)** — Highest priority academic target. Director of the Furman Center which is THE academic center for housing data research. Has published extensively on LIHTC placement and neighborhood effects. The cross-linked dataset enables research questions she's been unable to answer.

**Emily Benfer (Columbia Law)** — Leading housing rights researcher. Has published in JAMA on housing and health connections. REAC inspection data linked to management companies enables her policy advocacy work.

**Rachel Garshick Kleit (Ohio State)** — Studies LIHTC management company quality specifically. This dataset is almost custom-built for her research agenda.

### Media Contacts
**Heather Vogell (ProPublica)** — Highest priority media target. Has published major investigations on corporate landlords and housing quality. The management company accountability angle is directly relevant to her beat.

**Binyamin Appelbaum (NYT)** — Editorial board member covering housing policy. The "956 failing properties" finding is a strong news hook.

**Will Parker (WSJ)** — Covers real estate and affordable housing. The management company performance data reveals industry patterns.

### Strategic Partners
**NCSHA (National Council of State Housing Agencies)** — Represents the state agencies that allocate LIHTC credits. Management company performance data could inform allocation decisions.

**Enterprise Community Partners** — Largest LIHTC syndicator ($72B+ invested). Property quality cross-links enhance portfolio monitoring. Andrew Jakabovics leads policy development.

**NLIHC (National Low Income Housing Coalition)** — Uses HUD data for advocacy. Failing property data supports their affordable housing quality campaign.

## Approach Strategy

1. **Lead with the 956 failing properties** — This is the strongest news hook and the clearest demonstration of the dataset's value.

2. **Management company accountability** is the unique angle — Nobody else has built portfolio-level REAC score profiles for housing management companies. This is the core value proposition.

3. **For researchers**, emphasize the cross-linking methodology — The technical approach of connecting three separate HUD datasets is a methodological contribution in itself.

4. **For media**, provide sample records of worst-performing companies — Redacted enough to require licensing the full dataset, but revealing enough to demonstrate journalistic value.

5. **For clients**, focus on ROI — Compliance monitoring firms can save investigation time; syndicators can avoid bad management companies; software companies can enhance their products.
